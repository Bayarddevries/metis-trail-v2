1. Narrative Voice

The game currently reads like a rulebook. These sources have a distinct voice — part travelogue, part survival guide, part wonder at the landscape. The game should sound like a person who was there.

Before — Generic
"You encounter rough ground. The cart takes 1 wear. Continue?"
After — First-Person Voice
"The prairie breaks into a maze of gopher holes and sun-cracked clay. Your cart lurches and the axle groans — that sound you've learned to dread. One of the oxen stumbles. You'll lose half a day if you stop to check the wheel, but pushing on could mean a broken axle out here where there's no wood heavier than willow brush."
"The cart gave forth a blood-curdling squeal which could be heard for miles... caused by the friction of the dry wood of the hub turning on the axle. If grease was used the dust would mix with it and wear down the axle." — Brehaut, "Red River Cart and Trails" (1972), after Alexander Henry's 1801 account

Technical Approach

  • Rewrite all event text in first person, past tense, as if the player is recounting the journey
  • Use period vocabulary: "horse-flesh" for livestock, "cart-brigade" for groups, "the Settlement" for Red River
  • Add sensory detail: the squeal of cart wheels, alkali water taste, mosquito swarms at dusk, the smell of prairie fire smoke

2. The Cart Squeal — New Mechanic

Every source mentions the infamous squeal of ungreased cart wheels. Brehaut describes how it "could be heard for miles." This isn't just flavor — it's a gameplay mechanic waiting to happen.

What It Does

  • Alert radius: Squealing carts are visible/audible to NWMP patrols, whiskey traders, and buffalo from further away
  • Buffalo attract: On the prairie, squeal = herds visible from further off (hunting opportunity)
  • NWMP detection: Squealing cart doubles your detection range by patrols
  • Wear indicator: Squeal gets worse as wear increases — audio/UI cue that the cart needs repair

Repair Mechanics

  • Grease wheel: Uses 1 shaganappi. Eliminates squeal for 3 segments.
  • Replace axle: Uses 1 spare axle + 1 hour. Eliminates squeal permanently.
  • Emergency repair: Felloe binding with soaked rawhide strips (Brehaut: "When the dowel connecting two sections of the felloe broke... bound together with strips of animal hide... wound crosswise"). Temporary — lasts 1 segment.
"When loaded it gave forth a blood-curdling squeal which could be heard for miles and which came to be associated with it. This squeal could not be eliminated. If grease was used the dust of the trail would mix with the grease and wear down the axle or congel and prevent the wheel from turning." — Brehaut, "Red River Cart and Trails"

3. Mosquitoes & Environment

Marbot's account is consumed by mosquitoes. "Our mosquito and gnat miseries culminated." This is a constant theme in trail narratives — not just a nuisance but a genuine threat to health and sanity.

New Environmental Hazards

New Event — Mosquito Siege
🦟 Night of the Mosquitoes
"They rose from the marsh in clouds — a living fog that got into everything. Eyes, ears, the nostrils of the horses. One of the mules rolled in the shallows until it nearly drowned just to get a moment's relief. Sleep is impossible. The tea tastes of mosquito repellent and desperation."
🔥 Burn green wood for smoke
Requires firewood. Reduces mosquito penalty next 2 days. Risk: prairie fire (5%).
🧴 Apply mud and grease
Free. Crew morale -1. Reduces mosquito penalty 1 day.
🏕 Move camp to higher ground
Loses half a day. No mosquito penalty tomorrow. Wears cart on rough terrain (+1 wear).

4. NWMP Encounters

The NWMP research report details their trail presence. Marlborough customs duties and liquor enforcement. These become in-game encounters at forts and patrol points.

New Event — NWMP Patrol
🐎 Red Coats on the Trail
"Red-coated riders materialize from the heat shimmer — an NWMP patrol out of Fort Carlton. Their sergeant eyes your cart with the practiced look of a man who's searched a hundred just like it. 'Where you headed? What's in the cart? Any spirits?' His hand rests on the saddle holster."
🤝 Full compliance
Open cart, answer all questions. Honest travelers: no penalty. Smugglers: goods confiscated.
💬 Build rapport (Charisma DC 10)
Mention shared connections, offer tobacco. Reduced search intensity. Success = they wave you through. Failure = they search harder.
🛣️ Claim Métis passage rights
Métis weren't legally subject to the pass system. DC 12 Diplomacy. Success: they back down. Failure: detention and full search.

NWMP Fort Checkpoints

  • Fort Carlton: Major crossing point. Search for liquor and stolen goods. Biweekly patrols on the trail.
  • Fort Ellice: Smaller detachment. Focus on customs from Montana border traffic.
  • Fort Qu'Appelle: "Substantial police station." Crossroads of multiple trails. Highest patrol density.
  • Liquor enforcement: Primary NWMP mandate 1878-1885. Cart trains searched for alcohol. Penalty: confiscation.

5. Landscape & Trail Verisimilitude

Marbot's account is a masterclass in prairie observation. The game's flavor text should reflect what travellers actually saw and felt.

"The next day, from the last of a range of high hills... we caught sight, for the first time, of the faint blue line in the northern horizon which marked the course of the Assiniboine. Between were innumerable lakes — some salt and some fresh — shallow ones fringed with green or black rushes, and deep ones wooded to the banks... Away to the right was a column of smoke, where the careless dropping of a match had set the prairie on fire." — Marbot, "To Red River and Beyond" (1875)

Trail Markers

Ross describes specific features the game should reference as the player passes them:

  • La Belle Vallée: "A valley like the deserted channel of a beautiful river — seas of long green grass filled where waters used to be"
  • Mouse River: The junction where Mouse River runs within a mile of the Assiniboine
  • Sandhill crane sightings: "Huge birds, delicious to eat... we could see their ungainly majesties stalking about on distant sand-hills"
  • Alkaline water: "Ruined the flavor of our tea and gave all our horses what Joseph called 'an elementary canal enlargement'"
  • Prairie fires: Smoke columns visible for miles. The grass is knee-high and tinder-dry in late summer.

Daily Travel Rhythm

Ross describes the brigade system: 10 carts per group, 3 drivers, overseer, 2 mph, 20-25 miles/day maximum. Rest on Sunday: "Then our trousers and morals were mended... we sang Old Hundred, Balerma, Dundee, Ward." This becomes the in-game rhythm — every 7th day you camp, mend, and recover.

7. The Buffalo Hunt as Major Event

Ross's account of the 1840 spring hunt is extraordinary: 1,630 people, 1,210 carts, 2-month expedition. This should be a major multi-day event chain, not a footnote.

New Multi-Step Event — The Buffalo Hunt
🦬 The Great Hunt
"News spreads through the settlement: the herds have been spotted to the west. A great hunt is being organized. Every able-bodied person with a horse and a cart is expected to go. Your crew is restless — they want to join."
🐎 Join the full expedition
Takes 2 months. Massive food reward (+50 to +100). Risk: injury, lost time, cart wear. Your crew morale +4.
🦌 Send a small party
Send 2 crew. 2-3 weeks. Moderate reward (+15 to +30). Risk: crew injured or lost for 3 weeks.
🚫 Decline — push on
No food reward. Crew morale -2 ("We should have hunted"). Continue journey.
"The picturesque procession stretched some five or six miles... There were sixteen hundred and thirty persons and twelve hundred and ten Red River carts in this expedition. It was estimated that a total of 1,089,000 pounds of meat were obtained on this hunt." — Ross, "Red River Settlement" (1856)

8. Social Encounters

The sources describe a social trail — other travellers, settlers, missionaries, whiskey traders. The game currently has no social dimension beyond the player and events.

New Encounter Types

  • Cart Brigade: Another group travelling the same direction. Option to join (shared safety, slower speed) or travel alone (faster but riskier).
  • Missionary: A padre offers to bless your cart and crew (+1 morale for 5 segments). If you donate tobacco: +2 morale.
  • Whiskey Trader: Offer to sell liquor (illegal). Buy = short-term morale boost, long-term penalty. Report to NWMP = relationship bonus with police.
  • Métis Patrol: Offer to guide you through a shortcut (DC 12, saves 1 day, risk +2 wear). Free for Métis characters.
"The man of science divided his time between Paul's Epistles and the compound microscope, and gave us lectures from the latter, which helped our exegesis of the former, giving us wiser eyes to see the wonderful works of God." — Marbot, describing a fellow traveller on the Sunday rest day
