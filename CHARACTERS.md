# The Métis Trail — Characters & Starting Scenarios

---

## Starting Scenarios

The player chooses one of four scenarios. Each determines the starting location, supplies, crew condition, and the specific pressures driving the journey home.

### 1. The Failed Hunt
**Start:** Deep in the Cypress Hills, late autumn
**Situation:** The buffalo didn't come. Three weeks of waiting and the herd passed far to the south. Your party is low on food, the first snow has fallen, and winter is setting in hard. You need to reach Winnipeg before the rivers freeze or the plains become impassable.
**Starting supplies:** Lean — some pemmican, limited ammunition, adequate clothing, one Red River cart in rough shape
**Starting pressure:** Starvation and cold. The clock is ticking.
**Unique event chain:** You encounter a small Cree band who also failed to find the herd. Do you share what little you have, or press on alone?

### 2. The Trading Run
**Start:** Fort Edmonton, early spring
**Situation:** You came north to trade furs at the HBC post. The deal went bad — the factor lowballed your pelts, a dispute turned ugly, and now you're leaving with barely enough to cover your expenses. Word has reached you that land surveyors are marking parcels back home. You need to get back before your family's river lot is claimed.
**Starting supplies:** Moderate — trade goods (devalued), some cash, a serviceable canoe, decent clothing
**Starting pressure:** Urgency and politics. The surveyors are coming.
**Unique event chain:** An HBC trader follows your party, claiming you stole goods. You didn't — but proving it is another matter.

### 3. The Escort
**Start:** Qu'Appelle Valley, mid-summer
**Situation:** You were hired to escort a Métis family to relatives in the south. The journey went smoothly until a fever swept through the family. Two died. The survivors turned back, and now you're stranded in unfamiliar territory with your pay halved and your reputation damaged. The long road home to Winnipeg stretches ahead.
**Starting supplies:** Fair — you were well-equipped for the escort, but you've lost some supplies caring for the sick
**Starting pressure:** Reputation damage and guilt. Some communities will have heard about the failed escort.
**Unique event chain:** A member of the family you escorted catches up to you — they say they have information that the deaths weren't natural. Poison, not fever.

### 4. The Round-Up
**Start:** Near the Montana border, early winter
**Situation:** You crossed south to retrieve horses and cattle stolen by whiskey traders. You got the animals back — but one of your crew was shot in the raid, and the whiskey traders have friends on both sides of the border. You're being followed. The fastest route home means crossing through territory where the NWMP are looking for you.
**Starting supplies:** Good — you have horses, some cattle, weapons, and food from the raid — but your injured crew member is deteriorating
**Starting pressure:** Pursuit and injury. You're being hunted, and someone in your party is dying.
**Unique event chain:** An NWMP patrol spots your party. They don't know about the raid — yet. But the injured crew member can't stay quiet when the fever spikes.

## Crew Characters

The player assembles a crew of 4 from the following 8 characters. Each has a name, backstory, skill profile, and a personal storyline that unfolds during the journey.

---

### 1. THE HUNTER — Emile Laroque

**Skills:** Survival ★★★, Navigation ★★, Craft ★
**Health:** Strong | **Morale:** Steady

Emile is 34, born on the Red River, raised on the hunt. He can track anything across open prairie and bring it down with a single shot — but he's watched the buffalo dwindle year after year, and it's hollowing him out. He doesn't talk much. When he does, people listen. He's lost two brothers to the disappearing herds — one to starvation, one to despair.

**Kinship bonus:** Emile knows half the hunters between here and Winnipeg. +2 on Survival checks when near Métis settlements.

**Personal storyline:** Emile is searching for his younger brother Marcel, who left the Red River two years ago and hasn't been heard from since. If you pass through certain settlements, Emile finds traces — a carved marker, a secondhand story, someone who saw him. Finding Marcel becomes Emile's reason to keep going.

**Breaking point:** If the party goes more than 5 days without food, Emile becomes withdrawn. He stops contributing to group decisions. He's seen this before.

**Michif phrases:** "Nitanis" (my daughter — used for younger female party members), "Monshee" (the hunt, the chase)

---

### 2. THE TRADER — Josette Fontaine

**Skills:** Trade ★★★, Diplomacy ★★, Navigation ★
**Health:** Hardy | **Morale:** Sharp

Josette is 41, a widow who took over her husband's trading connections after he died of pneumonia at Fort Garry. She knows every factor, every post, every back-room deal between here and the Selkirk Settlement. She's shrewd, occasionally ruthless, and fiercely protective of her crew. She carries a ledger that tracks every debt owed to her — and every favour she owes.

**Kinship bonus:** Josette's trading network means you get better prices at HBC posts and independent traders. Advantage on Trade checks in established settlements.

**Personal storyline:** Josette is carrying a letter from a dying HBC factor that proves the Company knowingly sold contaminated provisions to a Métis winter camp two years ago. She means to deliver it to someone who can use it. The HBC would very much like that letter to disappear.

**Breaking point:** If the party loses more than half its trade goods or supplies in a single event, Josette becomes frantic. She'll propose increasingly risky deals to recover.

**Michif phrases:** "Aen lii bon" (a good deal), "Lii maanzh" (the goods/supplies)

---

### 3. THE ELDER — Marie-Celeste Morin

**Skills:** Spirit ★★★, Craft ★★, Diplomacy ★
**Health:** Frail | **Morale:** Deep

Marie-Celeste is 67. She has survived three famines, two resistances, and the death of every elder who taught her. She is the keeper of stories, songs, and the old ways — and she knows that knowledge dies with her if she doesn't pass it on. Her body is failing. Her mind is not. She travels because she refuses to die away from the Red River, where her ancestors are buried.

**Kinship bonus:** Marie-Celeste's presence grants the entire party advantage on Spirit checks. Her stories can restore morale after devastating events — once per journey segment.

**Personal storyline:** Marie-Celeste is teaching the Spirit skill to the youngest party member as they travel. Each time she tells a story or performs a ceremony, that character gains +1 Spirit (up to a cap). But each ceremony costs Marie-Celeste health. She is literally giving herself to keep the knowledge alive.

**Breaking point:** If Marie-Celeste's health drops below 25%, she can no longer walk unassisted. The party must decide: carry her (slower travel, another crew member burdened), leave her at a settlement (she may not survive the wait), or push on.

**Michif phrases:** "Li peyoot" (the stories, the old tales), "Kaa-twayit" (it is so / this is the truth)

---

### 4. THE SCOUT — Jean-Baptiste "JB" Bruyere

**Skills:** Navigation ★★★, Survival ★★, Craft ★
**Health:** Quick | **Morale:** Restless

JB is 26, the youngest of the crew options, and the fastest. He can read weather in the sky, find water in dry coulees, and spot trouble before anyone else knows it's there. He ran dispatch for Louis Riel during the 1869 Resistance — not out of politics, but because someone needed to carry messages between the settlements. He's been running ever since.

**Kinship bonus:** JB's speed means the party can choose to push ahead (extra travel distance per day) at the cost of increased fatigue. He also grants advantage on Navigation checks in open terrain.

**Personal storyline:** JB is carrying a message — a sealed letter he was paid to deliver to someone in Winnipeg. He doesn't know what it says. He doesn't know who's waiting for it. But people have killed for less, and he's started noticing shadows on his trail.

**Breaking point:** If the party is pursued or ambushed more than twice, JB's restlessness turns to paranoia. He advocates splitting up, going faster, leaving people behind. Someone needs to talk him down.

**Michif phrases:** "Paayik" (one, alone — used when he's scouting ahead), "Nistam" (again, once more)

---

### 5. THE MAKER — Thomas "Tom" Lavallee

**Skills:** Craft ★★★, Survival ★★, Trade ★
**Health:** Sturdy | **Morale:** Patient

Tom is 38, the man you want when your cart wheel shatters, your knife breaks, or your shelter needs to hold against a prairie blizzard. He can make something from nothing — a fire from wet wood, a patch from scrap leather, a shelter from bones and hide. He's quiet, methodical, and endlessly resourceful. He lost his workshop in St. Norbert to a fire three years ago and hasn't rebuilt. The road is his workshop now.

**Kinship bonus:** Tom can repair damaged equipment without needing a settlement. He also reduces the supply cost of Craft actions by half. Advantage on Craft checks in all conditions.

**Personal storyline:** Tom is looking for his daughter, Celeste, who was taken by a missionary school two years ago. He's heard she might be in a school near Winnipeg. Every settlement is a chance to ask. Every church is a door he has to knock on.

**Breaking point:** If the party encounters a mission or residential school and is denied entry or information, Tom becomes volatile. He'll argue for forcing entry, confronting the clergy, doing something — anything. He's a calm man pushed to his edge.

**Michif phrases:** "Aen machin" (a tool, something made), "Kihtwam" (again, rebuilt — he uses this word a lot)

---

### 6. THE TALKER — Marguerite "Marge" Dumont

**Skills:** Diplomacy ★★★, Trade ★★, Spirit ★
**Health:** Vigorous | **Morale:** Fierce

Marge is 45, and she can talk her way into or out of anything. She negotiated a peace between two feuding Métis families at 19. She talked an NWMP sergeant into releasing her brother from custody at 23. She convinced a HBC factor to give her trade credit on a handshake at 30. She's not a liar — she's a listener. She finds what people need and gives them a reason to help her. She's also the descendant of Gabriel Dumont, and she carries that name with both pride and weight.

**Kinship bonus:** Marge can attempt to defuse any hostile encounter through Diplomacy. Even on a failed roll, she reduces the severity of the consequence. She also knows which communities will welcome the party and which won't.

**Personal storyline:** Marge is returning to Winnipeg because her nephew has been arrested on charges she believes are fabricated. She has documents proving his alibi — but the documents are in the hands of a priest in St. Boniface who may not want to hand them over.

**Breaking point:** If a Diplomacy check fails and results in violence, Marge blames herself. She withdraws for 2 days, unable to lead negotiations. The party must rely on others.

**Michif phrases:** "Aen nwayr" (a promise, a word given), "Lii maa" (the people, the community)

---

### 7. THE SPIRITUAL ONE — Father Auguste Mercredi

**Skills:** Spirit ★★★, Diplomacy ★★, Craft ★
**Health:** Worn | **Morale:** Troubled

Father Auguste is 52, a Métis priest ordained in St. Boniface — one of the first. He walks between two worlds that both claim him and neither fully accepts. The Church sees him as a tool for reaching the Métis; the Métis see him as a tool of the Church. He has performed last rites for people the Company let starve and baptized children whose land was being stolen. The contradiction is killing him. He carries a Bible and a medicine pouch. He uses both.

**Kinship bonus:** Father Auguste grants advantage on Spirit checks involving ceremony, prayer, or healing. His dual role means he can access both Catholic missions and traditional Métis ceremonies — opening doors no other character can.

**Personal storyline:** Father Auguste has received word that the bishop intends to reassign him to a remote northern mission — effectively silencing him. He's returning to Winnipeg to appeal the decision, but he's also questioning whether the Church is still his home. If he encounters traditional ceremony during the journey, the player can choose his path: double down on his faith, embrace the old ways, or try to hold both.

**Breaking point:** If the party encounters a situation where Church authority and Métis survival are in direct conflict (a mission refusing shelter, a priest demanding conversion for aid), Father Auguste must choose. His choice permanently shifts his skill profile — either losing Spirit access to traditional ceremony, or losing his Church-based Diplomacy advantage.

**Michif phrases:** "Mii sa" (it is finished / so be it — used in both liturgical and traditional contexts), "Pakos" (to break, to split — he uses this when referring to his divided loyalty)

---

### 8. THE FIGHTER — Isidore "Izzy" Beaulieu

**Skills:** Survival ★★★, Craft ★★, Navigation ★
**Health:** Tough | **Morale:** Defiant

Izzy is 29, and he's done being careful. He fought at Duck Lake — not as a leader, just as a man with a rifle who'd had enough. He lost friends there. He lost the illusion that patience would save his people. He's not reckless — he's precise, controlled, and angry. He can shoot, track, and survive anything the prairie throws at him. What he can't do is sit still while things get worse.

**Kinship bonus:** Izzy grants advantage on Survival checks in hostile or dangerous terrain. If the party is attacked, he can hold a defensive position alone, buying time for others to act.

**Personal storyline:** Izzy is carrying a list — names of Métis men who were killed or disappeared after the Resistance, and who is responsible. He's not seeking revenge. He's seeking witnesses. He wants the world to know what happened, and he's heading to Winnipeg because that's where the newspaper printers are.

**Breaking point:** If the party encounters NWMP or government officials who display cruelty or contempt toward Métis people, Izzy struggles to hold back. The player must roll Spirit to keep him from acting — and if he acts, the consequences are severe and immediate.

**Michif phrases:** "Aen taan" (a stand, a fight), "Nistam kitam" (we do this again — used with bitter irony)

## Recruitment Mechanics

When a crew member is lost (death, desertion, or separation), the party may find replacements at settlements, trading posts, or along the trail. Recruits are drawn from a pool of secondary characters with simpler profiles:

**Example recruits:**
- A young Cree woman fleeing a bad marriage (Survival, Navigation)
- An old HBC hand who quit and has nowhere to go (Trade, Craft)
- A Métis cart driver whose entire convoy was lost in a storm (Navigation, Craft)
- A widowed mother traveling with her child — you take both or neither (Spirit, Diplomacy)

Recruits start with lower skills than original crew members and no kinship bonuses. They haven't earned trust yet. Over time and through events, they can develop personal storylines and kinship — but never as deeply as the original eight.
