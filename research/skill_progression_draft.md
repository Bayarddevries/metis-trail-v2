# Draft: Skill Progression System for The Métis Trail RPG

**Time frame**: 1878 – 1885, Red River trail, 21 – 70 days. PC leads a Métis crew of 4.

---

## 1. Starting Crew Growth

- **Goal**: Original 4 characters evolve over the journey, reflecting the cumulative hardships and triumphs. Progress should feel tangible but not like a grinding grind.
-
### 1.1 Growth Model

We propose a **hybrid model** that blends:
-
1. **Use‑Based** – Every successful skill check (or learning from a failure) accrues a single **Progress Point** (PP).
2. **Milestone‑Based** – Major narrative events grant **Milestone Points** (MP): crossing a major river, surviving a blizzard, reaching a settlement, passing a critical negotiation.
3. **Time‑Based** – Recruits grow +1 star per 14 days. Original crew gain the same rate **only if** they start with a *lower* star rating than the recruits’ cap. For example, a 1★ worker can become 2★ after 14 days of “experience” (tracked via MP). 3★ is the ultimate cap for any crew member during a standard run.
4. **Narrative‑Based** – Side quests (personal storylines) grant a *bonusing* star once completed. These are optional but encourage role‑playing.

---

### 1.2 Growth Speed

- **Baseline**: 1 star per 14 days of travel.
- **Maximum**: 3 stars for any skill in any crew member. The cap keeps the game balanced and allows the story to focus on interpersonal dynamics rather than endless progression.
- **Potential**: A starting 2★ character can reach 3★ once a milestone or narrative triggers occur *early*, or if they accumulate many use‑based points. If a character starts at 1★, they can reach 3★ after roughly 42 days + two milestones.
- **No 5★**. A 5★ star would give an unfair advantage, breaking the survival focus. Skill ratings only up to 3★.

---

## 2. Growth Triggers

### 2.1 Skill Use
- **Successful Use** – +1 PP to that skill.
- **Failed Use** – +1 PP if the failure was *intentionally* pushed or if the narrative outcome was a **Learning Moment** (e.g., a near‑miss that revealed a better route). A failed check that ends the journey in jeopardy does *not* yield progress (because the crew suffered).

### 2.2 Difficulty Scaling
- **Challenge Rating**: Continue using the existing DC tiers (Easy 8, Moderate 12, Hard 16, Extreme 20). A successful *Hard* or *Extreme* check yields **double** the PPs (2 PP) to reflect tougher learning.
- **Danger Level**: For checks directly tied to survival (e.g., crossing a flooded river), a *hard* success is worth 3 PP. An *extraordinary* success (21+) could net 5 PP.

### 2.3 Milestones
- **Major Milestones**: Crossing a primary river, surviving a blizzard, negotiating a safe passage with settlers, reaching a major supply post.
- **Reward**: 5 MP, which convert to 2 PP in a relevant skill (e.g., a negotiated safe passage grants 2 PP in Diplomacy). MPs are spent only once per character.

### 2.4 Narrative Side‑Objectives
- Completed personal story arcs grant a **Bonus Star** in the related skill *once*. For example, Josette’s “prove her worth” arc grants +1 Diplomacy star after the first successful trade that secures supplies.

---

## 3. NARRATIVE PROGRESSION

| Milestone or Side‑Objective | Skill Gained | Relationship Impact |
|---|---|---|
| First river crossing | 2 PP in Navigation | Strengthens kinship with Navigators (high morale bonus)
| Surviving the first blizzard | 2 PP in Survival | All crew receive a short morale boost
| First successful barter at a trading post | 2 PP in Trade | New trade partners may offer a bonus to future trades
| Completed personal arc (e.g., Marge calming a dispute) | +1 star in Diplomacy | Unlocks a unique dialogue option for future negotiations

---

## 4. CREW SYNERGY

- **Teaching**: A crew member can *teach* another. If a character with ≥ 2★ in a skill spends a day teaching a lower‑star crew member, the teacher gains a bonus **Teaching Bonus**: 1 extra PP on the next skill check they make, and the student receives 1 PP for learning.
- **Synergy Bonus**: If two crew members share a primary skill (e.g., both have Craft ≥ 2★), every successful check made conjointly (e.g., joint repairs) grants 1 PP to each participating skill.
- **Sidenote**: Teaching is a *choice* and consumes one day off travel; it should be logged in a “Crew Development Log” kept by the storyteller.

## 6. FEEL

- **Growth as Experience**: Progress is tracked in *points* earned from real events. The crew’s star rating reflects the *collective weathering* of the journey, not arbitrary increments.
- **Narrative Integration**: Milestones are narrated by the game master; each skill progression is announced in a way that feeds into the story:
  - *“Emile thanks you for the trade—his Survival skill shines brighter. He now has a new star.”*
- **Balance**: The 3★ cap ensures that players cannot become super‑heroic; the focus remains on decision-making, resource management, and role‑playing.
- **Teacher‑Student Dynamic**: Characters who share skills become mentors—this encourages players to pick complementary crews and to invest time in teaching moments.

---

### Implementation Checklist

1. Add **Progress Tracker** to each character sheet (stars + PP). Notation: `★` for star rating, `PP (X)` for progress points.
2. Create a **Crew Development Log** template for the GM to fill daily/after milestones.
3. Define **Milestone Trigger Table** (see Section 3) to standardize which events give MPs.
4. Update **Staff Table** to include potential teaching bonuses.
5. Document **Heroic Failure** rules: catastrophic failures do *not* yield PP.
6. Ensure the **Survival & Morale** systems are updated to reflect learning effects (e.g., Survival PP may reduce exhaustion thresholds).

---

**TL;DR:** Original crew grow at the same rate as recruits (1★ / 14 days) but can pick up extra *progress points* from successful or difficult skill uses, major journey milestones, and completing personal side quests. Growth caps at 3★. Teaching and synergy bonuses give optional extra progress, reinforcing the theme that the journey itself trains the crew.
