# Town Stay Mechanics — The Métis Trail RPG

> **Design Context:** Historical survival RPG, Red River cart trails, 1878–1885. The player leads a Métis crew overland toward Winnipeg. Towns are waypoints — necessary for resupply, dangerous for lingering. Currency is the **Made Beaver (MB)**, the HBC trade standard.

---

## 1. Action Economy

Each town day provides **3 action slots** (morning, afternoon, evening). Some actions consume 1 slot; a few consume 2. The crew acts as a unit — the player allocates slots, not individual character turns.

### Action Definitions

#### 🔄 Trade (1 slot)

**What it does:** Buy, sell, or barter goods at the settlement's available markets. The GM determines available inventory based on settlement type (see §3). Prices are listed in MB.

- **Sell furs/pemmican/etc.** — Convert cargo to MB at the local rate. Rates vary by settlement (HBC Forts pay in MB credit; Trading Posts pay cash but lower rates; Métis communities offer fair trade but limited MB liquidity).
- **Buy supplies** — Ammunition, food staples, cloth, twine, kettles, etc. Availability depends on settlement type and size.
- **Barter** — Trade goods directly for goods (no MB changes hands). Some NPCs prefer barter; some settlements operate largely on barter (Métis Road Allowance Communities).
- **Special orders** — At HBC Forts or large posts, the player may request unusual items (surveyor's tools, specific ammunition, cart hardware). These arrive on the **next visit** to that settlement, not this one.

**Mechanical note:** A single Trade action covers one buying session and one selling session. The player may Trade more than once per day to access different merchants or markets.

---

#### 🛏️ Rest (1 slot)

**What it does:** Recover crew Stamina and reduce Wear. Staying in town is the primary way to heal.

- **Standard Rest:** Recover 2 Stamina per crew member. Reduce Wear on one cart by 1 (basic maintenance while resting).
- **Full Rest (2 slots):** Recover 4 Stamina per crew member. Reduce Wear on all carts by 1. Requires lodging (see §5).
- **No Lodging Rest:** If the crew camps outside town (no MB spent on lodging), recover 1 Stamina only. No Wear reduction. Risk of random encounter at night (roll on event table with disadvantage).

**Mechanical note:** Rest without lodging is possible but weak. Towns incentivize paying for proper shelter.

---

#### 🔧 Craft / Repair (1 slot)

**What it does:** Fix carts, craft gear, or modify equipment.

- **Cart Repair:** Reduce a cart's Wear by 2 per slot spent. Requires raw materials (lumber, rawhide, iron) — if the crew doesn't have materials, they must buy them via a Trade action first, or pay 2 MB per point of Wear reduced (covers materials + labor at the settlement's rate).
- **Craft Gear:** Make items from available materials — snowshoes, leather goods, fishing tackle, repair clothing. The GM sets the DC based on item complexity. Success produces the item; failure wastes half the materials.
- **Modify Cart:** Add or remove modifications (outrunner skis, cargo shelving, reinforce axle). Each modification takes 1 slot and requires materials or 3 MB.

**Settlement variation:** HBC Forts have full workshops. Métis communities have skilled makers but limited iron. NWMP Posts have farrier tools only. Trading Posts have basic repair capacity. Missions have limited craft options (sewing, some woodworking).

---

#### 🕵️ Gather Intel (1 slot)

**What it does:** Learn about the trail ahead, local conditions, political developments, and opportunities.

- **Trail Conditions:** Learn weather forecasts, river crossing status, buffalo herd locations, band activity on the next trail segment. The quality of information depends on the settlement's position and who you talk to.
- **Political News:** Learn about NWMP patrols, land survey activity, government proclamations, Scrip distribution schedules, tensions between factions.
- **Rumors:** Roll on the settlement's rumor table. May produce side objective hooks, warnings, or red herrings.
- **Map the Route:** If the crew has no map of the next segment, spending a slot gathering intel can produce a rough sketch — grants +1 Navigation on that segment.

**Settlement variation:** NWMP Posts are excellent for political news. Trading Posts are best for trail conditions (travelers pass through constantly). Métis communities know the land intimately. Missions hear confessions — they know secrets.

---

#### 🗣️ Social (1 slot)

**What it does:** Engage with NPCs — recruit, negotiate, resolve storylines, build relationships.

- **Recruit:** Attempt to hire a new crew member. Availability depends on settlement type. The player must make a Persuasion or Reputation check. Success: the recruit joins (terms negotiated). Failure: they decline or demand higher pay.
- **Negotiate:** Re-negotiate crew wages, settle debts, make deals with local figures. Can reduce ongoing costs or open new opportunities.
- **Storyline Progression:** If an active storyline has a node in this settlement, a Social action advances it. Some storylines require a specific NPC to be present.
- **Build Relationship:** Spend time with a specific NPC to improve their disposition toward the crew. May unlock future favors, discounts, or information.

**Settlement variation:** Every settlement has Social potential, but the *type* of encounter varies wildly. See §3.

---

#### 🌿 Spirit / Ceremony (1 slot)

**What it does:** Perform or participate in Métis cultural and spiritual practices. This is not a "buff" system — it is about maintaining identity, morale, and connection to the land and community.

- **Smudge / Prayer:** A brief personal or small-group ceremony. Restores 1 Spirit per participating crew member (Spirit is a meta-resource that resists cultural erosion and despair). Costs nothing but time.
- **Community Ceremony:** Available only in Métis Road Allowance Communities and some Mission settlements (if the priest is sympathetic). A feast, a fiddle dance, a naming. Restores 2 Spirit to all crew, improves community disposition by 1, and may trigger a storyline beat.
- **Vision Quest / Sweat:** Available only in Métis communities or at specific sacred sites near certain settlements. Consumes 2 slots (the whole day). Restores 3 Spirit, grants a narrative vision the GM weaves into the story. Risk: if the crew is being pursued or time is critical, this is a real cost.

**Design note:** Spirit/Ceremony is intentionally not "efficient" in action-economy terms. It competes with Trade and Repair. That tension is the point — the Métis way requires ceremony, but the trail demands progress. The player must choose.

---

#### ⚖️ Deal with Authorities (1 slot)

**What it does:** Interact with institutional power — the NWMP, HBC factors, Indian Agents, government surveyors.

- **Answer Questions:** If the NWMP or HBC has taken interest in the crew (see Soft Pressure §2), this action may be *mandatory* — consuming a slot whether the player wants it to or not.
- **Present Scrip / Papers:** If the crew has legal documents (Scrip, trading licenses, travel passes), presenting them can reduce NWMP scrutiny, unlock restricted areas, or speed border crossings.
- **Pay Fines / Settle Accounts:** Resolve outstanding legal issues. Costs MB but clears the crew's name.
- **Negotiate with HBC Factor:** At HBC Forts, the Factor controls the trade terms. A successful negotiation can improve buy/sell rates for this visit. A failure makes them worse.

**Settlement variation:** Mandatory only at NWMP Posts and HBC Forts. At other settlements, it's optional. At Trading Posts, "authorities" may mean the post owner's private rules.

---

## 2. Soft Pressure to Leave

There is no hard turn limit. The crew *can* stay indefinitely. But by day 3, every system is quietly (then loudly) pushing them out. The pressure is cumulative and narrative — the world moves on without them.

### Day 1: Full Welcome

- All actions available at full effectiveness.
- Lodging is affordable (base rate).
- NPCs are friendly and interested — "New arrivals! Where are you headed?"
- Markets are stocked.
- **Morale:** Normal.

### Day 2: The Bill Comes Due

- Lodging costs are now **real** — the player feels the MB drain (see §5).
- Some market inventory is picked over. The best furs are sold, the choice ammunition is gone. Remaining stock is standard or overpriced.
- NPCs are still friendly but no longer fascinated: "Still here? I thought you were heading out."
- Side objectives begin ticking: if a trader said "I need those goods by tomorrow," tomorrow is now.
- **Morale:** -1 if the crew has no stated plan to depart.

### Day 3+: The Walls Close In

Cumulative effects that stack each day beyond the second:

#### 📉 Crew Morale Erosion

Each day past Day 2, crew Morale drops by 1. When Morale hits certain thresholds:

| Morale Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| 0 to -1 | Crew grumbles. No mechanical penalty yet, but NPCs comment on it. |
| -2 | **Desertion risk.** At the end of each town day, roll 1d6 for each crew member. On a 1, they leave — hired by a local, found other work, simply walked away. The player loses that crew member. |
| -3 | **Mutiny.** Crew refuses to stay. If the player tries to remain, they must make a Leadership check (DC 14) or the crew departs without them — the player wakes up to empty carts and a note. |

Morale can be patched with Spirit/Ceremony actions or successful Social actions, but that costs action slots — slots that aren't being spent on the reason the crew is in town.

#### 💸 Stacking Costs

- **Lodging** increases by 1 MB per crew member per day past Day 2 (the innkeeper knows you're stuck; rates go up).
- **Stabling/cart parking** costs add up (1 MB per cart per day at most settlements).
- **Food** — eating in town costs more than trail rations. 1 MB per crew member per day beyond what the crew's own stores cover.

#### ⏳ Expiring Side Objectives

Any time-limited side objective (delivery, message, rendezvous) advances its clock each day. The GM should have at least one side objective active per town visit with a 2–3 day window. Staying past the window means **failure** — lost reputation, lost MB, lost storyline branches.

#### 🔁 Nothing New Appears

After Day 2, no new random events fire (see §4). No new NPCs arrive. No new rumors. The information well is dry. The town becomes static — it has nothing left to offer.

#### 🚔 Growing NWMP / Authority Attention

Each day past Day 2, the crew's **Scrutiny** score with the local authorities increases by 1:

| Scrutiny Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | An officer casually asks where you're headed. No roll required, but it's noted. |
| 2 | **Mandatory "Deal with Authorities" action.** The NWMP wants to inspect your cargo, check your papers, ask questions. This consumes one of your 3 daily slots — whether you want it to or not. |
| 3 | Curfew imposed on the crew. No evening actions. Effectively, you have 2 action slots per day. |
| 4 | **Detention.** One crew member is held for questioning for 1d2 days. They cannot take actions. |
| 5 | The NWMP issues a formal order: leave within 24 hours or face arrest on vagrancy charges. This is the hardest the pressure gets — it's still not a game-over, but resisting means a confrontation. |

Scrutiny resets to 0 when the crew departs and returns after at least 2 trail segments.

**Design note:** Scrutiny is the "hard edge" of the soft pressure system. It's the only mechanic that directly reduces player agency (by consuming slots, imposing curfews, detaining crew). It kicks in late and escalates slowly, giving the player plenty of warning. The threat of Scrutiny 2+ is usually enough to make Day 3+ feel genuinely uncomfortable.

## 3. Settlement Type Variation

Not all towns are created equal. The North-West Territories in 1878–1885 contain a patchwork of settlement types, each with distinct options, risks, and narrative flavor.

---

### 🏰 HBC Fort (e.g., Fort Edmonton, Fort Carlton)

**The deal:** Company towns. The Hudson's Bay Company runs everything — trade, law, social hierarchy. The Factor is king. You trade on their terms or you don't trade.

**Available Actions:**

| Action | Availability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Trade | ✅ Full | Best prices for furs (HBC sets the rate — fair but non-negotiable without a Deal with Authorities slot). Good stock of manufactured goods, ammunition, hardware. |
| Rest | ✅ Full | Company barracks available for hire. Clean, dry, expensive. |
| Craft/Repair | ✅ Full | Company workshops — blacksmith, carpenter, wheelwright. Full service, but you pay HBC labor rates (2 MB per point of Wear reduced if using their materials). |
| Gather Intel | ⚠️ Limited | The HBC controls information. Political news is filtered. Trail conditions are decent (the Company has couriers). Rumors are scarce — people don't gossip freely under the Factor's eye. |
| Social | ⚠️ Limited | Social life is stratified. Officers don't mix with cart haulers. Recruitment is limited to Company employees between contracts — skilled but expensive. |
| Spirit/Ceremony | ❌ None | No ceremonial space. The Company tolerates Catholic services in the chapel (if the crew wants to attend Mass, that's a Social action, not a Ceremony). Métis ceremonies must happen outside the palisade — effectively, not available as an action. |
| Deal with Authorities | ✅ Full | The Factor *is* the authority. Negotiating trade terms, requesting special orders, settling accounts. |

**What's NOT available:**
- Métis ceremony or cultural practice
- Barter-based trade (HBC deals in MB or credit, not barter)
- Black-market or grey-market goods
- Free social mixing across class lines

**Risks:**
- HBC debt spiral: if the crew trades on credit and can't pay, the Factor can seize cargo or impound carts.
- Information control: the HBC may not tell the crew about dangerous trail conditions, NWMP activity, or political developments that affect them.
- **Bonus:** HBC Forts are the safest settlements. No random theft events inside the palisade. Disease risk is low. NWMP Scrutiny does not apply here (the Company handles its own security).

**Unique Hooks:**
- The Factor offers a "Company contract" — guaranteed prices and protection in exchange for exclusivity (the crew must sell all furs to HBC). Breaking this contract has consequences.
- A Company clerk has been skimming — he approaches the crew with an off-books deal.
- The Fort is expecting a supply ship/carfvan that's overdue. Tension is high. The Factor asks the crew to investigate.

---

### 🏘️ Métis Road Allowance Community (e.g., St. Albert, St. Laurent, Batoche)

**The deal:** Home. These are the crew's people — extended family, old friends, people who share the language, the faith, the way of life. But these communities are poor, squeezed by surveyors and land grants, and everyone is struggling.

**Available Actions:**

| Action | Availability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Trade | ⚠️ Limited | Mostly barter. Little MB liquidity — the community operates on mutual obligation and trade goods. What MB exists is tied up in essential supplies. Prices are fair but inventory is thin: basic food, pemmican, some ammunition, handmade goods. No manufactured hardware unless a trader just passed through. |
| Rest | ✅ Full | Stay with family. **Free lodging** (no MB cost) — but you owe a social debt. If you don't reciprocate (gifts, labor, sharing supplies), community disposition drops. |
| Craft/Repair | ⚠️ Limited | Excellent hide-workers and woodworkers. Cart repair is possible but **iron is scarce** — axle repair costs an extra 1 MB (or equivalent barter) for iron. Can craft: leather goods, snowshoes, spruce bark containers, clothing. Cannot craft: metalwork, complex hardware. |
| Gather Intel | ✅ Full | **Best settlement type for trail knowledge.** The Métis know the land, the rivers, the buffalo movements, the safe routes. +2 to any Navigation or trail-info check. Political news is community-sourced — unfiltered, passionate, sometimes exaggerated. |
| Social | ✅ Full | Rich social encounters. Family reunions, old rivalries, community politics, marriage negotiations, Council meetings. Recruitment is easy — many young people want to join a crew heading out. **Bonus:** recruits from Métis communities start with +1 Loyalty (they're among kin). |
| Spirit/Ceremony | ✅ Full | **Full ceremony access.** Smudge, community feast, fiddle dance, sweat lodge. The only settlement type where Spirit/Ceremony is always available and always effective. Community Ceremony grants +1 community disposition in addition to Spirit restoration. |
| Deal with Authorities | ❌ None | There are no authorities here. That's the point. (If NWMP show up, that's a random event — see §4.) |

**What's NOT available:**
- Large-scale trade in MB
- Manufactured goods (iron, guns, ammunition in quantity)
- Institutional authority (no one to "deal with" — the community governs itself)

**Risks:**
- **Emotional weight:** Staying too long in a struggling community is *hard*. The player sees poverty, hears about land disputes, watches children go hungry. Morale drops by an additional 1 per day past Day 2 if the crew hasn't shared supplies (a voluntary MB cost).
- **Obligation trap:** Free lodging creates social debt. If the crew leaves without giving gifts or helping with labor (consuming an action slot on "Community Work"), community disposition drops — and the next visit is colder.
- **NWMP raids:** Métis communities are under surveillance. Roll on the random event table with +2 to NWMP-related results.

**Bonus:**
- Free lodging (with social obligation)
- Best trail intel
- Best ceremony
- Easy recruitment with Loyalty bonus

**Unique Hooks:**
- A family asks the crew to carry a message (or a person) to Winnipeg — a side objective with emotional weight.
- The Council is debating whether to resist the surveyors. The crew's presence influences the debate.
- A elder wants to send sacred items east for safekeeping — will the crew carry them?
- Someone is selling Scrip applications on behalf of people who don't understand what they're signing. The crew can intervene — or exploit it.

---

### 🏛️ NWMP Post (e.g., Fort Walsh, Battleford, Fort Macleod)

**The deal:** The law. The North-West Mounted Police represent Ottawa's authority — young, disciplined, sometimes idealistic, sometimes abusive. The crew is watched from the moment they arrive.

**Available Actions:**

| Action | Availability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Trade | ⚠️ Limited | The NWMP aren't merchants. A small canteen sells basics (tea, tobacco, hardtack, some ammunition). Prices are fair but selection is thin. No fur purchasing — the Mounties don't buy pelts. |
| Rest | ✅ Full | Barracks-style lodging available. Clean, austere, 1 MB per person per day. |
| Craft/Repair | ⚠️ Minimal | Farrier services only (horse-related). A wheelwright may be on staff at larger posts (Battleford, Fort Walsh). Cart repair at half normal rate (1 point of Wear per slot, not 2). No crafting beyond basic repairs. |
| Gather Intel | ✅ Biased | Excellent political and legal information — NWMP know what Ottawa is doing, where patrols are headed, what laws are being enforced. **Terrible** trail info — most Mounties have never traveled the cart trails the way the Métis have. Rumors are thin and official. |
| Social | ⚠️ Tense | Social encounters are possible but strained. The NWMP are not the crew's community. Recruitment is limited to deserters (dangerous) or civilian staff (cooks, interpreters). Some officers are sympathetic; others are hostile — the player won't know which until they engage. |
| Spirit/Ceremony | ❌ None | No ceremonial space. The chapel (if any) is Anglican or non-denominational Protestant — Métis Catholic practices are tolerated but not accommodated. No ceremony actions available. |
| Deal with Authorities | ✅ Full | **This is the point of NWMP Posts.** The player can: present papers, answer questions, pay fines, request travel permits, file complaints (usually futile), or negotiate patrol schedules (if the crew wants to avoid a specific patrol on the trail). |

**What's NOT available:**
- Fur trade
- Métis ceremony
- Rich social encounters
- Blacksmithing / full craft

**Risks:**
- **Scrutiny starts at 1 on arrival.** The NWMP are watching. Day 1 already includes a casual inspection. Day 2+ escalates faster than other settlements (add +1 to all Scrutiny gains).
- **Cargo inspection:** The NWMP may demand to inspect carts. If the crew is carrying anything questionable (unlicensed furs, stolen goods, contraband), this is a problem.
- **Recruitment danger:** Hiring a deserter means the NWMP will come looking for them — adding Scrutiny to *every future settlement* until resolved.

**Bonus:**
- Best legal/political intel
- Safe from non-authority threats (no theft, no bandit raids while at the post)
- Can obtain official travel documents (reduce future Scrutiny at other NWMP Posts by 1)

**Unique Hooks:**
- An NWMP officer privately asks the crew to carry a report to the next post — they don't trust their own couriers. Accepting means cooperating with the law; refusing means making an enemy.
- A prisoner in the stockade is Métis — the community asks the crew to advocate for them (or break them out).
- The post is understaffed and the commander offers paid work: escort a patrol, deliver dispatches, track a fugitive. Good MB, but it costs time and loyalty from the crew.

---

### 🏚️ Trading Post (Neutral Ground) (e.g., Various independent posts along the trail)

**The deal:** Neither Company nor Crown. Independent trading posts are run by freelancers — retired voyageurs, Métis entrepreneurs, American whiskey traders, or opportunists of every stripe. They exist where the HBC and NWMP haven't reached (or have been driven out of).

**Available Actions:**

| Action | Availability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Trade | ✅ Full | **The whole reason these places exist.** Buy and sell freely. Prices are market-driven — the post owner sets rates based on supply, demand, and how desperate you look. Furs sell well. Manufactured goods are available but irregular — whatever the last trader brought in. **Barter welcome.** Sometimes the best deals are off-book. |
| Rest | ✅ Adequate | Bunkhouse or floor space. 1 MB per person per day. Not comfortable but dry. |
| Craft/Repair | ⚠️ Basic | The post owner can arrange repairs if there's a handyman around. 1 point of Wear per slot, standard material costs. Crafting limited to what the local talent can do — unpredictable. |
| Gather Intel | ✅ Excellent | **Best settlement for unfiltered trail news.** Everyone passes through trading posts — Métis, Cree, Assiniboine, American traders, fugitives, surveyors, missionaries. Rumor tables are rich. Political info is unreliable but plentiful. Trail conditions come from people who just walked them. |
| Social | ✅ Unpredictable | Anyone could be here. Recruits, fugitives, storytellers, con artists, lost travelers, couples eloping. Social encounters are the most varied and volatile at trading posts. Roll on a dedicated "Trading Post Social" table. |
| Spirit/Ceremony | ⚠️ Informal | No formal ceremony space, but if the crew asks, the post owner may allow a smudge or prayer in a back room. Restores 1 Spirit (not 2). No community ceremony possible — there's no community here. |
| Deal with Authorities | ❌ None | No authorities — that's the selling point. (If NWMP show up, it's a random event, and the post owner is *very* unhappy about it.) |

**What's NOT available:**
- Institutional authority
- Formal ceremony
- Reliable craft/repair
- Consistent inventory (the GM should randomize stock each visit)

**Risks:**
- **Theft.** Trading posts have the highest theft risk of any settlement type. Roll on the random event table with +3 to theft results. The post owner doesn't guarantee your gear.
- **Bad deals.** The post owner is a hustler. Prices may be inflated. Counterfeit goods are possible. The player should Gather Intel before Trading to get a sense of fair prices.
- **Violence.** Disputes at trading posts are settled personally — no law, no appeal. If a deal goes sour, it can escalate to a fight.
- **Whiskey.** Some trading posts are de facto whiskey traders (especially near the Cypress Hills). Crew morale may *increase* temporarily (easy access to alcohol) but Spirit *decreases* by 1 per day — drinking numbs the pain but erodes the way.

**Bonus:**
- Best trade flexibility (buy, sell, barter, off-book deals)
- Best unfiltered intel
- No authority oversight
- Most varied social encounters

**Unique Hooks:**
- The post owner offers a "map to a buffalo jump" — for a price. Is it real?
- An American trader is buying furs at 150% of HBC rates — but paying in US currency, not MB. Converting it back costs 20% at an HBC Fort.
- A Cree band is camped nearby. They want to trade but don't trust the post owner. The crew could act as intermediaries.
- Someone left a locked box here for "the next Métis crew heading east." It's addressed to the player's family name.

---

### ⛪ Mission Settlement (e.g., St. Boniface, Île-à-la-Crosse, Lac la Biche)

**The deal:** Faith, education, and the complicated legacy of the Church in Métis communities. Missions are run by Catholic (usually Oblate) priests and Grey Nuns. They offer shelter, schooling, medical care, and spiritual guidance — all filtered through a colonial lens. Some missionaries are genuine allies; others are instruments of assimilation.

**Available Actions:**

| Action | Availability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Trade | ⚠️ Minimal | The Mission isn't a store. They may have a small stock of basic supplies (flour, salt, cloth, medicine) sold at cost. They do **not** buy furs or trade goods. However, they often have a **mission store** run by lay brothers that operates like a small Trading Post (limited inventory, fair prices, MB only). |
| Rest | ✅ Full | The Mission offers shelter freely — the Christian duty of hospitality. **Free lodging** for up to 2 nights. After that, the priest expects the crew to contribute (labor, donation, or MB). |
| Craft/Repair | ⚠️ Limited | The Mission has carpenters and seamsters (brothers and nuns). Can repair cart bodies, sew canvas, make clothing. **No ironwork.** Cart repair: 1 point of Wear per slot, but only for non-metal components. |
| Gather Intel | ⚠️ Mixed | Priests hear confessions — they know secrets. Political intel is available but church-filtered (the Mission knows what the Bishop knows, which is significant but biased). Trail info is weak — priests don't travel the trails. However: **medical knowledge** is best here. If the crew has sick or injured members, the Mission is the place to treat them (1 slot = full treatment, no MB cost). |
| Social | ✅ Distinct | Mission social encounters are unique: orphans seeking guardianship, families torn between traditional and church life, priests wrestling with conscience, nuns running the hospital. Recruitment: limited, but orphans and displaced people may be available. |
| Spirit/Ceremony | ⚠️ Complicated | **Catholic Mass** is available and restores 1 Spirit (familiar ritual, comforting to Métis Catholics). **Métis ceremony** is possible only if a sympathetic priest turns a blind eye — a Persuasion check (DC 12). Success: full ceremony in secret, 2 Spirit. Failure: the priest forbids it; attempting it anyway risks being asked to leave. |
| Deal with Authorities | ❌ None | The Mission has no secular authority. However, the priest can **write letters of introduction** to other Missions along the trail — effectively a safe-passage document in church networks. |

**What's NOT available:**
- Fur trade
- Metalwork
- Reliable political intel
- Unconditional Métis ceremony

**Risks:**
- **Cultural friction:** If the crew practices Métis ceremony openly, a strict priest may report them to the NWMP or deny them shelter. The player must navigate the tension between faith and tradition.
- **Proselytizing pressure:** Each day past Day 1, the priest makes a "suggestion" — attend Mass, send the children to school, sign a Scrip application "for their own good." Each suggestion the crew refuses costs 1 community disposition with the Mission. Each one accepted costs 1 Spirit (conceding to assimilation, even partially, hurts).
- **Orphan subplot:** The Mission always has orphans. If the crew has any empathy at all, this is a emotional hook that costs time, MB, or both.

**Bonus:**
- Free lodging (2 nights)
- Free medical treatment
- Letters of introduction (safe passage at other Missions)
- The confessional = secrets

**Unique Hooks:**
- A child at the Mission recognizes a crew member — "You're my uncle." A family separated by the road system.
- The priest has been secretly protecting a wanted Métis leader in the rectory. The NWMP are coming.
- The Grey Nuns need medicine from Fort Edmonton. Will the crew carry it? (Side objective: free, but the trail isn't safe for a detour.)
- A spiritual crisis: the oldest crew member wants to stay at the Mission and "retire from the trail." Do you let them go?

## 4. Random Town Events (d20)

Roll once per town day during the evening phase (after actions are resolved). Some events are modified by settlement type — notes in brackets.

| d20 | Event | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Thief in the Night** | A random piece of cargo or equipment is stolen. The player may spend a Social action the next day to investigate. **Trading Post:** auto-trigger instead of rolling; this happens. |
| 2 | **Recruit Approaches** | A capable stranger offers to join the crew. Roll on the Recruitment Table for their skills and terms. **Métis Community:** +1 Loyalty, blood relation possible. **NWMP Post:** 50% chance the recruit is an informant. |
| 3 | **NWMP Patrol Arrives** | An NWMP detachment rides in. Scrutiny increases by 1 for the remainder of the stay. **Métis Community:** tension rises — community disposition drops by 1. **Trading Post:** the post owner hides things. |
| 4 | **Festival / Celebration** | A community event — feast, dance, games, horse races. All crew members restore 1 Spirit and 1 Stamina. **Métis Community:** also +1 community disposition. **HBC Fort:** only if the Factor authorizes it (50% chance). |
| 5 | **Disease Outbreak** | An illness spreads through the settlement. Any crew member who didn't Rest this day must make a Constitution check (DC 12) or fall Sick (Disadvantage on all physical actions until treated at a Mission or with medicine). **Missions:** the outbreak is contained quickly — only affects 1 crew member. **Trading Posts:** no sanitation — +2 to DC. |
| 6 | **Trader's Special Deal** | An itinerant trader offers a one-time deal: surplus ammunition, rare goods, or a map at 50% off. Available for 1 day only. If the player doesn't Trade that day, the deal is gone. |
| 7 | **Dispute Erupts** | A violent argument breaks out between locals — two families, traders, or factions. The crew can intervene (Social action next day, DC varies) for reputation gain, or stay out of it. If they stay, there's a 1-in-6 chance the dispute spills into their camp — random crew member takes 1 damage. |
| 8 | **Bad Weather Rolls In** | A storm, deep cold, or flooding hits the settlement. Departing the next day requires a Survival check (DC 13) or the crew must wait an extra day. **All settlements:** this event can trigger at any type. |
| 9 | **Old Debt Collectors** | Someone the crew owes (or someone who owes the crew) appears. Resolve the debt: pay MB, fight, negotiate, or dodge. If the crew owes and can't pay, lose 1 Reputation and the debt escalates. |
| 10 | **Spiritual Sign** | An unusual natural event — northern lights, a strange animal encounter, a vivid dream. One crew member of the player's choice gains 1 Spirit. If the crew performs a Ceremony the next day, it restores +1 extra Spirit. |
| 11 | **Supply Caravan Arrives** | A trade convoy reaches the settlement. Market inventory is refreshed and expanded — buy rare goods that weren't available before. This is the only event that can make Day 3+ trading viable again. **HBC Fort:** guaranteed Company goods. **Trading Post:** random mixed inventory. |
| 12 | **Crew Conflict** | Two crew members argue over route, leadership, or personal matters. Morale drops by 1. The player must spend a Social action to mediate, or the conflict festers (further -1 Morale the next day, and both members get Disadvantage on Cooperation checks). |
| 13 | **Lost Traveler** | A desperate stranger arrives — sick, wounded, or simply lost. Helping them costs 1 MB in supplies and 1 action slot but grants +1 Reputation. Ignoring them costs 1 Spirit (the crew knows they turned away someone in need). |
| 14 | **Raid Warning** | Rumor of a raiding party (Cree, Assiniboine, or outlaws) in the area. The settlement goes on alert. Departure is riskier — the next trail segment has a mandatory Encounter check. Staying means the settlement may be attacked (GM's discretion — this can become a major storyline event). |
| 15 | **Gambling Opportunity** | A card game, horse race, or other wager is organized. The player may bet MB. Roll 1d6: 1–2 lose the bet, 3–4 break even, 5–6 win double. **Trading Post:** always available; add option to bet cargo. **NWMP Post:** gambling is forbidden — if caught, Scrutiny +2. |
| 16 | **Letters from Home** | Mail catches up with the crew — letters from family, official documents, news from Winnipeg. No mechanical effect, but the GM uses this to deliver narrative hooks: a parent is ill, a land claim is being challenged, a child was born. The player may choose to alter their route or priorities based on this information. |
| 17 | **Price Fluctuation** | Market prices shift. Roll 1d6: 1–2, fur prices drop 25% this visit. 3–4, normal. 5–6, fur prices rise 25%. **HBC Fort:** the Factor sets the price — this event is always bad for the crew (1–4 = drop, 5–6 = normal, never a rise). |
| 18 | **Scrip Agent Arrives** | A government Scrip commissioner sets up a table. Métis crew members may apply for Scrip (land or money). The process is dehumanizing — long lines, confusing paperwork, low valuations. Applying costs 1 action slot and 1 Spirit. The payout is 20–40 MB (roll 2d4 × 5). Refusing to apply is fine, but other community members may pressure the crew. **Métis Community only; reroll at other settlements.** |
| 19 | **Cart Breakdown** | One of the crew's carts develops a problem — cracked axle, loose wheel, torn canvas. Wear increases by 1 on the affected cart. If the player doesn't Craft/Repair the next day, the cart cannot travel until fixed. |
| 20 | **Quiet Night** | Nothing happens. The crew rests undisturbed. All crew members restore 1 extra Stamina from the peaceful evening. This event cannot occur on Day 3+ — the pressure system ensures something always goes wrong by then. Reroll if rolled on Day 3+. |

### Event Modification by Settlement Type

| Settlement Type | Modifications to d20 Table |
|---|---|
| HBC Fort | Event 3 (NWMP) → reroll (NWMP doesn't patrol inside Company walls). Event 1 (Theft) → reroll. Event 18 (Scrip Agent) → reroll. |
| Métis Community | Event 3 (NWMP) → triggered on 3 *or* 18 (Scrip Agent is always NWMP-adjacent here). Event 4 (Festival) → +1 Spirit, +1 Morale. |
| NWMP Post | Event 1 (Theft) → reroll. Event 5 (Disease) → reroll (military hygiene). Event 15 (Gambling) → if rolled, Scrutiny +1 instead of normal gambling effect. |
| Trading Post | Event 1 (Theft) → always occurs on a 1–2 (not just 1). Event 6 (Special Deal) → +25% better deal. Event 3 (NWMP) → if rolled, the post owner gives the crew 1 MB each to "not mention certain items." |
| Mission | Event 5 (Disease) → severity reduced (DC drops to 10). Event 18 (Scrip Agent) → reroll. Event 1 (Theft) → reroll. Event 10 (Spiritual Sign) → +1 extra Spirit. |

## 5. Lodging Costs

Lodging is per crew member per night. Cart stabling is per cart per night (where applicable). Costs are in MB.

| Settlement Type | Lodging (per person/night) | Cart Stabling (per cart/night) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HBC Fort** | 2 MB | 1 MB | Company rates. Non-negotiable. Includes a meal. |
| **Métis Community** | 0 MB | 0 MB | Free — stay with family. But: **social debt** accumulates. After 2 free nights, the crew must reciprocate (gift 5+ MB worth of goods, or spend 1 action slot on Community Work) or lose 1 community disposition. |
| **NWMP Post** | 1 MB | 1 MB | Barracks rate. Includes tea and hardtack. No privacy. |
| **Trading Post** | 1 MB | 1 MB | Bunkhouse rate. Mattress on the floor. Meals extra (1 MB/person/day if the crew doesn't cook their own). |
| **Mission** | 0 MB (2 nights) | 0 MB | Free for 2 nights — Christian hospitality. Night 3+: 1 MB/person (the priest gently suggests a donation). The crew can pay or perform labor instead. |

### Staying Past Day 2: Cost Escalation

| Day | Lodging Modifier | Additional Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Base rate | — |
| Day 2 | Base rate | — |
| Day 3 | Base rate + 1 MB/person | "Extended stay" surcharge (or, at Métis Communities, the social debt becomes urgent) |
| Day 4+ | +1 MB/person per day beyond Day 2 (cumulative) | Day 4 = +2, Day 5 = +3, etc. The settlement knows you're stuck and prices reflect it. |

**Example:** At an HBC Fort, lodging for a 6-person crew costs:
- Day 1: 12 MB (6 × 2)
- Day 2: 12 MB
- Day 3: 18 MB (6 × 3)
- Day 4: 24 MB (6 × 4)

By Day 4 at an HBC Fort, the crew has spent 66 MB on lodging alone. That's real money — enough to buy a fully loaded cart.

## Appendix: Quick Reference — Town Stay Flow

1. **Arrive.** Note settlement type. Set Scrutiny to 0 (unless NWMP Post → starts at 1).
2. **Each town day:**
   - Player allocates 3 action slots (morning, afternoon, evening).
   - Resolve actions.
   - Roll on Random Town Events table (evening).
   - Pay lodging costs.
   - Check Morale. If Day 3+, apply morale penalty and Scrutiny increase.
3. **Depart.** Reset town-specific trackers. Carry social debts, Scrutiny reputation, and storyline progress forward.
