1. Economy Rework

Every game ends in starvation by day 10-14. The economy must create interesting scarcity, not inevitable death. Players should feel the tension of hard choices — not realize on day 3 that the math doesn't work.

Food Economy Flow

The player should arrive at settlements with a meaningful decision: "I have 8 food left and 3 segments to the next settlement. Do I push on (risking starvation) or rest here to recover?"

  • Travel: -1 food/day (unchanged). The baseline cost.
  • Make Camp: 0 food (was -1). Camping is free but doesn't move you forward or heal crew.
  • Settlement — Rest: +2 food (settlement provisions). Costs 1 day but recovers crew.
  • Settlement — Trade: +6-10 food. Costs 1 trade good (your win condition).
  • Settlement — Forage: +1-4 food (Survival DC). Free but risky — crew exhaustion on failure.

Target Win Rate

After tuning, target 30-50% win rate for an informed player. Current: 0%. Games should be winnable but require smart resource management. Most losses should feel like "I made a bad call" not "the numbers were impossible."

2. Settlement Depth

Settlements are currently "Rest or Continue." They should be the most interesting decisions in the game — the places where you scramble for supplies, weigh tradeoffs, and feel the weight of your choices.

New Settlement Actions

Settlement Types

Different settlement types offer different action sets:

  • ⛪ Mission: Rest (+2 food), Heal (injuries), Gather Rumours. No trade.
  • 🛖 Métis Settlement: Rest, Trade, Forage. Best forage bonuses.
  • 🏪 Trading Post: Trade (better rates), Repair, Recruit crew. No free rest.
  • 🏰 HBC Fort: Full services. Best trade rates but expensive repairs.

Trade Goods System

Trade goods are your win condition. Each one is unique with a description:

  • Bison Hides (×3): +8 food or -2 wear when traded
  • Beaver Pelts (×2): +10 food or learn next event
  • Shaganappi (×3): +6 food or repair for free
  • Pemmican (×20): +2 food each (emergency rations)

You need ≥1 trade good at Fort Edmonton to win. Spend them to survive, but spend too many and you can't win.

4. Crew System

Crew currently has no personality — just names on a list. Players should care about their crew, make decisions to protect them, and feel the weight of losing someone.

New Crew Mechanics

  • Health: Healthy → Injured → Critical → Dead. Injured on failed event rolls. Heal at missions or with rest.
  • Morale: Good → Steady → Low → Breaking. Affects roll bonuses. Low morale = risk of desertion at next settlement.
  • Skills: Each crew member has a unique skill that gives +2 to relevant rolls. Lose the bonus if injured.
  • Story Events: Certain crew trigger unique events. Gabriel Dumont: "I know a shortcut (DC 14, saves 2 days, risk breakdown)." Old Michel: "Let me tell a story around the fire (+2 morale, costs 1 day)."

5. Event Variety & Multi-Step Events

Events are currently one-choice-immediate-resolution. Some events should chain — a first choice opens up new choices. Some should have partial successes. Some should be avoidable entirely with the right crew or trade goods.

The Cart button currently shows a boring list. Make it a spatial grid like Resident Evil 4's attaché case — items take different sizes, and you must manage limited space.

Inventory Mechanics

  • Grid system: Items take 1×1 or 2×1 slots. Forces meaningful choices about what to carry.
  • Weight limit: 400 lbs max. Overweight = +1 wear per day, crew tires faster.
  • Item rarities: Trade goods (large, valuable), Supplies (medium, consumable), Tools (small, reusable)
  • Combining: Drop two items to combine (e.g., repair kit + shaganappi = free repair)
