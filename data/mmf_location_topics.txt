Summary
This document outlines a set of potential topic areas to feature within an interactive map of the Métis Homeland. The intent is to support users as they explore the map by providing context that explains Métis origins, the meaning of “Homeland,” and why specific places, routes, events, and individuals are culturally and historically significant. The proposed topics are designed to balance geography with story – linking land to kinship, movement, language, economy, governance, and resistance, so users come away with a clearer understanding of how Métis identity and community were shaped across this territory and why the Homeland continues to matter today.


For open panels outlining summary details within the map experience, it would be ideal to keep the character count to around 650 characters or less.
Trails as a means to link locations
* Cart trails and York boat trails identify well-travelled areas
* The communities or settlements that fall upon these trails
* Link to fur trade
* RR Métis dispersal and the routes that were taken (tie to buffalo hunt)
* Emphasis on flora and fauna and how it impacted navigation
* Variation in language between locations
